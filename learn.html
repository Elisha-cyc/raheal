What is Climate Change?

Climate change refers to significant, long-term changes in the global climate. The global climate is the connected system of sun, earth and oceans, wind, rain and snow, forests, deserts and savannas, and everything people do.

The climate has always varied naturally, but the term climate change is now commonly used to refer to the recent and rapid changes caused by human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels which releases greenhouse gases into the atmosphere.

Effects of Climate Change

Rising Temperatures

Global temperatures are increasing, leading to more frequent and intense heatwaves.

Sea Level Rise

Melting glaciers and thermal expansion of oceans are causing sea levels to rise.

Extreme Weather

More intense hurricanes, droughts, and heavy precipitation events.

Ecosystem Changes

Shifting habitats and loss of biodiversity as species struggle to adapt.

Causes of Climate Change

Main Human Causes

  • Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas)
  • Deforestation
  • Industrial processes
  • Agriculture (especially livestock)
  • Waste management

Solutions to Climate Change

Transitioning from fossil fuels to renewable energy sources like solar, wind, and hydroelectric power can dramatically reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Many countries are already making significant progress in this area.

Improving energy efficiency in buildings, transportation, and industry can reduce energy demand. This includes better insulation, LED lighting, and more efficient appliances and vehicles.

Expanding public transit, promoting electric vehicles, and creating bike-friendly cities can reduce emissions from transportation, which is a major contributor to climate change.

Protecting existing forests and planting new trees helps absorb CO2 from the atmosphere. Deforestation accounts for about 10% of global emissions.

Practices like regenerative farming, reduced meat consumption, and better soil management can reduce emissions from agriculture while improving food security.
